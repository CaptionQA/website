CaptionQA

Is Your Caption as Useful as the Image Itself?

About CaptionQA

CaptionQA is a utility-based benchmark for evaluating image captions across real-world domains. Unlike traditional metrics that focus on text similarity, CaptionQA measures whether captions can stand in for images in downstream tasks.

The benchmark covers 4 domains—Natural, Document, E-commerce, and Embodied AI—with 33,027 multiple-choice questions over 657 images (averaging 50.3 questions per image). Each question tests specific visual information defined by domain-specific taxonomies (25 top-level and 69 subcategories).

Our evaluation reveals substantial gaps: state-of-the-art MLLMs lose 9-32% utility when moving from image to caption, with models that perform similarly on traditional benchmarks showing dramatically different caption utility.

Key Findings

  • Utility Gap: Even the strongest proprietary models (GPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro) lose 9.2-16.4% utility when captions replace images.
  • Open-source models show larger gaps (11-32.4%), with Embodied AI domain exceeding 40% gap.
  • Hidden Discrepancies: Models nearly identical on traditional image-QA benchmarks can diverge by up to 32% on caption utility.
  • Domain Variation: Caption quality varies significantly across domains—what works for natural images may fail for documents or e-commerce.

Four Real-World Domains

Natural Images: Emphasizes object properties, spatial relations, and hallucination detection (6 top-level, 22 subcategories).

Document: Targets layout, content, and document-specific structure for automation pipelines (6, 15).

E-commerce: Focuses on product attributes and presentation for recommendation systems (7, 16).

Embodied AI: Captures perception, spatial understanding, and task-relevant cues for robotics (6, 16).

Domain Taxonomies

CaptionQA taxonomies define the visual information that captions must carry to be useful for downstream tasks across four domains.

Benchmark Construction Pipeline

We use a rigorous multi-stage pipeline to generate high-quality, utility-focused questions. Starting from human-designed taxonomies and curated images, we generate questions using multiple MLLMs, then filter and refine through (1) embedding-based deduplication, (2) text-only blind test to remove questions answerable from priors, (3) dual-VLM quality control, and (4) human refinement.

The Utility Gap: Image vs. Caption

Evaluating state-of-the-art MLLMs reveals a consistent gap between QA-on-image and QA-on-caption performance. This gap directly measures how much utility is lost when captions replace images in downstream tasks.

Example Questions

Each question in CaptionQA is grounded in the image and explicitly requires visual information to answer. Questions are tied to specific taxonomy nodes, ensuring comprehensive coverage of domain-specific utilities.
